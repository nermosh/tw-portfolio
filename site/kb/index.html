Knowledge Base Management

During my ten years at Technical Support department at Intermedia Communications, I was contributing to their knowledge base in many ways. At first I was only writing articles which followed the style guide to address feedback or questions received from customers via Support channels. Later I took part in Knowledge Management projects as assistant to a knowledge manager, later fully took over knowledge manager responsibilities.

The Knowledge Base Goals and Specifics

The knowledge base I worked with covered about 20 products and over 1,300 articles at max. Some products came from the aquired companies and therefor articles covering such products were not aligned with knowledge base syle guide and formatting which caused troubles.

White Label Strategy

The knowledge base served a white-label strategy covering five different brands, with different set of products available for each. The KMS hosted the content with variables and published it on five different sites replacing them with branded product names and using any parts that were unique for each brand.

The Knowledge Management System (KMS) tool

The Knowledge Base at first was using a simple self-hosted core for hosting and publishing articles, supporting variables for white-label strategy.

Challenge

The authoring tool had a simple and very limited WYSWYG editor.

  • While articles native to this knowledge base were displaying fine, the articles migrated from other systems as part of company acquisition often had various issues with searchability or display.
  • Some articles needed to use a custom formatting, which is supported by the system, but not offered in the authoring tool.

Achievement

I worked with the source HTML code of articles and corrected the HTML tags to align with the formatting acceptable for the KMS.
