# Document Types

My work included a wide range of materials:

- **Hardware quick start guides**: Using vendor’s guides for desk phones and PDF documents created by the marketing operations team, wrote the articles with enhanced formatting adapted for machine translation and AI adoption.
- **How-to Guides**: Developed comprehensive how-to guides covering installation, application usage, and system management, empowering users to effectively utilize our products.
- **Product Landing Pages**: Developed and maintained engaging landing pages for the Support Center, ensuring easy navigation and access to critical information.
- **Release Notes**: Proofread, enhanced, and published notes provided by Project managers.
- **Customer-Focused Video Tutorials**: Produced engaging video tutorials that simplify complex technical concepts and provide step-by-step instructions for common tasks.

---

## Goal-Oriented How-To Guides

These articles are designed to help users achieve a specific task as quickly as possible. This includes installation, application usage, and system management.

!!! info "Business Goal"
    To reduce the volume of basic support tickets by empowering users and admins to handle setup and management independently.

!!! warning "Obstacles and Challenges"
    !!! quote "My Role"
    Lead Technical Writer and Content Creator.

!!! success "What I did to achieve it"
    I developed a comprehensive library of step-by-step guides. I focused on clear, action-oriented headings and used a consistent structure so that users could skim the document and find the specific "how-to" step they needed without reading the entire page.

---

## Product Landing Pages (Explanation)

Before a user can "do," they need to "understand." These pages serve as conceptual hubs for our products.

!!! info "Business Goal"
    To provide a high-level overview of product ecosystems, ensuring users understand how different features work together before they dive into technical tasks.

!!! warning "Obstacles and Challenges"
    Information was often fragmented across different departments. I had to consolidate technical details from Product Owners and Marketing into a single, cohesive "source of truth."

!!! quote "My Role"
    Knowledge Manager / Information Architect.

!!! success "What I did to achieve it"
    I designed and maintained landing pages that acted as a "map" for the Support Center. By organizing content conceptually, I improved site navigation and ensured that critical information was only one or two clicks away for any given product.

---

## Technical Specifications & Hardware (Reference)

These are "fact-based" documents, such as hardware quick start guides for desk phones and software release notes.

!!! info "Business Goal"
    To provide reliable, searchable technical facts that are easy for both humans and AI-driven support tools to process.

!!! warning "Obstacles and Challenges"
    Original vendor guides were usually static PDFs that were difficult to search and didn't play well with machine translation (MT) or AI tools.

!!! quote "My Role"
    Technical Writer and Editor.

!!! success "What I did to achieve it"
    I reformatted hardware guides into web-native articles. I used "clean" formatting specifically optimized for machine translation and AI adoption. For release notes, I translated raw technical updates from Project Managers into user-friendly summaries that highlighted key fixes and new features.

---

## Visual Learning (Tutorials)

For complex configurations, sometimes a video is more effective than a long text document.

!!! info "Business Goal"
    To simplify high-level technical concepts (like mail protection policies) for visual learners, reducing the perceived complexity of the product.

!!! warning "Obstacles and Challenges"
    Identifying which topics actually needed video support required deep-diving into customer feedback and support trends.

!!! quote "My Role"
    Video Producer and Scriptwriter.

!!! success "What I did to achieve it"
    I produced a series of tutorials by analyzing feedback to identify "pain points." I handled the entire pipeline: storyboarding, writing scripts, coordinating voiceovers with native speakers, and final video editing (including closed captions) to ensure the content was accessible to everyone.
